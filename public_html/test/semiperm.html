SEMIPERM

All tenants in Semiperm receive project-based Section 8 rental subsidies from the government. However, unlike most transitional housing units, Semiperm requires tenants to pay their remaining share of the rent directly, either through their work paycheck or their monthly shelter allowance. They also pay directly for their electricity and are encouraged to open up bank accounts. Each head of household also signs a social contract requiring them to either work, attend college or enroll in a full-time vocational training program during their time at Semiperm. The contract also mandates that each parent attend on-site programs offered bt social workers and others.

To further promote the tenants' self sufficiency, Semiperm will run on-site, after-school programs for children and teach classes in parenting, nutrition, health care, and financial literacy. One social worker will meet with each resident on a weekly basis, using task-centered counseling. Periodically, goals will be reviewed and new ones added.

Settlement Housing Fund will supplement the on-site classes by partnering with other neighborhood program providers including daycare, employment training, physical and psy- chological health services, credit and legal assistance. We are confident that the combination of our on and off-site services and the close personal attention and support of our staff will enable residents to gain and apply new skills that will facilitate their transition to permanent housing within 5 years.

Upon graduating Semiperm, residents will have priority for vacancies in Settlement Housing Fund’s permanent housing developments. Successful graduates will also be given hous- ing choice vouchers. We believe they will also be attractive canidates for units in other affordable housing projects should they wish to live in a neighborhood where Settlement Housing does not have apartments. Wherever our residents choose to move, we will work closely with neighborhood program providers and the schools to help ensure their successful transition.
