DREAMS
(Dedicated Responsible Educated Accountable Motivated & Self-Sufficient)

Settlement Housing Fund DREAMS/Youthbuild program offers a much needed resource in Bedford Stuyvesant, helping disconnected youth gain the skills to help them back onto the road of gainful employment and higher education, while providing wrap-around social, psychological, and childcare to help them overcome obstacles of poverty that may have originally derailed them. The DREAMS' YouthBuild program imparts both formal education and construction skills through the use of interactive, hands-on models of learning. The long-term goals of the DREAMS YouthBuild program consist of each participant’s attainment of a General Education Diploma (GED) and subsequent enrollment in a post-secondary education program and/or securing of gainful employment. Educationally, the program offers:

*GED instruction by a New York State Certified Teacher
*A hands-on, classroom and shadowing program teaching construction maintenance skills
*Career planning and activities, and
*Training on life skills such as time management, financial literacy, and anger management. All lessons are reinforced through computerized learning, inter-disciplinary assignments and daily journal writing to give each student an opportunity to review and reflect upon what s/he has learned.



Out of 23 participants who enrolled in DREAMS’ first session (which began in January 2007) we retained 18 to graduation – an outstanding retention rate for this type of program. These young people have all shown vastly increased confidence, skill and commitment to self-improvement. Nine earned their GED’s after before or soon after their June graduation. Over the summer, DREAMS was rewarded $35,000 from the Fund for the City of NY’s Community Education Pathways for Success Program, which has recently enabled us to add a pre-GED track to our program which accommodates the nearly 50 percent of our applicants who do not have the requisite reading and math skills to enroll in our original program. Then, in September, Settlement Housing Fund learned it was chosen as a winner of a nation wide competition, and will be receiving receive $1.9 million dollars from the federal government for the GED component of our DREAMS program. The funds will be allotted by the federal government over a period of three years, with Settlement Housing Fund receiving $548,188 for 2008. DREAMS is now operating in its second school year, with 27 young adults enrolled in our Pre-GED track, 23 enrolled in the DREAMS/Youthbuild GED and job-training program, and 8 taking the original DREAMS GED program. Of DREAMS students past and present GED students, 20 have secured their GED, and of these, eight have gone on to study at local colleges. Our next graduation ceremony will take place on June 26, 2008.
